My Projects.

During my time at university and in my work placements I have had the chance to take on many projects. I have had a lot of fun on these projects and displaying them here allows me to show what I have done.

Dissertation – Creating and Improving the Enigma Machine.

During my final year at Hull University I was required to complete a Dissertation project. I chose to develop an Enigma Machine in JavaScript and improve the core weakness where an Enigma could never encipher a letter as itself (Improved Enigma). This would be checked to ensure security against the cribbing method exploited by the bombe in WW2. The project would then compare the Enigma’s to modern encryption standards like AES. This project scored very highly with an overall grade of 81%, the project and full write up are available here for any intrigued readers.

Rolls-Royce - Creating a Evidence Database.

During my time at Rolls-Royce I experienced a lot of different areas of Cyber Security, however I also was able to make a proof of concept tool for the forensics department. The forensics department didn’t have a good way of keeping track of all their evidence coming in and out of the lab and using Excel had gotten very confusing. So, I coded a web portal with a database using PHP. The solution was quite simple, but it helped them a lot and this will be the start of properly digitalising the forensics department at Rolls-Royce.

Fujitsu - Creating a Risk Management Tool.

While at Fujistu I was tasked with creating a tool to be a response to the demise of IS2. This tool took in Risks, Threats, Vulnerabilities and Controls and compared them all to generate a series of risk metalanguage statements and rate the risk from “very high” to “low”. This tool was not meant to replace a risk assessment but to assist in a full risk assessment. This tool was entirely coded in C# as this was the language, I was studying at University at the time. In hindsight this probably would have been better as a web application and future placement students had the task of porting the tool.

Computational Science – Predicting Movements of a Robot.

Computational Science was the module I scored highest in at University scoring 88%. I was tasked to create a machine learning perceptron to predict the movements of a robot in 1 Dimensional space. To do this I implemented a series of different algorithms, Euler’s Method was used to find the values of the robot at different step sizes, Box Muller algorithm was used to add “noise” values to the data so that the perceptron could be more accurate, then finally I coded the perceptron using both Step and sigmoid activation functions. The full project and write up can be found here on my GitHub profile. .
